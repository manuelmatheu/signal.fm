## Design Context

### Users
Music nerds who obsessively track their listening, but also want passive discovery without curation effort. The creator is the primary user. Context: leaning back, scrolling, listening — not managing or configuring.

### Brand Personality
**Fresh, wonder, joyful.** The interface should feel like stumbling onto a great record you didn't know existed. Light, alive, a little surprising. Never heavy, never admin-like.

### Aesthetic Direction
Neon Pulse: deep purple-void dark mode, warm orange accent, cinematic ambient album art atmosphere. DM Serif Display for display type, Manrope for body. The UI should feel like a high-end editorial music magazine fused with a late-night listening session — not a dashboard, not a playlist manager.

**Anti-reference**: Generic AI slop — symmetrical card grids, cyan-on-dark gradients, glassmorphism used decoratively, everything centered, every section with an icon above a heading.

### Design Principles
1. **Discovery, not management** — UI surfaces music, not controls. Reduce chrome, increase content.
2. **Intentional asymmetry** — break the safe centered grid; let typography and content breathe unevenly.
3. **Fresh wonder over productivity** — interactions should feel like flipping a record, not clicking a button.
4. **No redundancy** — if something can be inferred or found elsewhere, remove it. Every element earns its place.
5. **Cinematic first** — the ambient album art layer is the soul of the product; never let chrome compete with it.
